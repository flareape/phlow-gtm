# Phlow — DM Playbook
### 7 message types · ready to send · not templates

> **The rule:** No pitch. No features. No "I built an app." Let the product speak.  
> The only job of a DM is to get one install and one honest reaction.  
> Follow-up once, 3–5 days later, if no reply. Then stop.

---

## Before you send anything: build the list

Go through your contacts (WhatsApp, Instagram, LinkedIn, Telegram) and put 20 names into this framework:

| Name | Relationship | Context | Message type to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| ___ | Close friend | Tracks her cycle, works for herself | Type 1 |
| ___ | Founder acquaintance | You've met 2–3x, she's building something | Type 2 |
| ___ | Someone who said "I'm so tired" recently | Doesn't need to track her cycle to identify | Type 3 |
| ___ | Has MCAS / POTS / endo (you know this) | **Check if regular cycle first** — if irregular, use Type 5 with caveat | Type 5 |
| ___ | Freelancer / no employer / solopreneur | Her schedule IS her health | Type 6 |
| ___ | In a women founder community with you | You interact there but don't know her personally | Type 7 |

> ⚠️ **V1 fit check before you send:** Does she likely have a regular cycle (28–32d)? If not — PCOS, perimenopause, hormonal pills without a period, post-hysterectomy — be upfront in the message. Do not chase conversion; chase honest signal.

Fill in 3–4 names per type. That's your 20.

## The 7 message types

---

### Type 1 — Close friend / trusted contact
*Use for: women you actually talk to regularly. The most casual version.*

> Hey — I've been building something weird and you're one of the first people I thought to try it.  
> It's called Phlow. One question, tells you what to work on today based on your cycle.  
> Takes 10 seconds. Tell me if it's accurate?  
> [https://phlow.work]

**Why it works:** "One of the first people" is honest and personal. "Tell me if it's accurate?" is a real question, not a pitch. The ? invites a reaction, not a download action.

---

### Type 2 — Founder acquaintance (warmer)
*Use for: women you know through work or founder circles, 2–3 prior conversations.*

> Question for you — you ever feel like your brain works completely differently in different parts of the month? Not just energy, but like actual thinking style?
>
> I built something around that. It's called Phlow. Free, no account, 10 seconds to get your card.  
> Curious what you think: [https://phlow.work]

**Why it works:** Opens with a question she can answer yes to before you've mentioned a product. Creates context for why the product makes sense. Curiosity, not pitch.

---

### Type 3 — Founder acquaintance (less warm / semi-cold)
*Use for: women you know of, maybe one prior interaction, or in a shared group.*

> Hi [Name] — reaching out because I think you'd have an honest reaction to something I built.
>
> It's a work-planning tool that maps to your hormonal cycle. Takes 10 seconds, completely free, no account. The science behind it is actually solid — based on a 2022 study on cognitive performance across the cycle in real workplace settings.
>
> Would love to know if it resonates or if it misses: [https://phlow.work]

**Why it works:** "Honest reaction" signals you want critique, not validation. Citing real science pre-empts the wellness-skeptic reaction. "Misses" shows you're not precious about it.

---

### Type 4 — Someone who mentioned burnout / energy crash recently
*Use for: any woman who said something like "I'm exhausted", "I don't understand my energy", "I'm not consistent".*

> This is going to sound like an odd reply to [what you said about X], but — what you described is actually well-documented in the neuroscience around hormonal phases.
>
> I built something that tries to make that actionable day-to-day. It's called Phlow.  
> Free, one question, tells you what your brain is wired for today.  
> Might explain some of it: [https://phlow.work]

**Why it works:** References a real prior conversation. Validates her experience before mentioning a product. "Might explain some of it" is honest — it may or may not, and that's fine.

---

### Type 5 — Woman with MCAS / POTS / endometriosis
*Use for: women you know have a chronic condition where cycle phase matters. **Caveat: v1 only works with a regular cycle. If she has irregular periods, flag it upfront — do not set wrong expectations.***

> Hey [Name] — I built something that I think might actually be useful for you, though I know everyone's experience is different.
>
> It's a daily work-planning card that maps to your cycle phase — which tasks fit which hormonal window, what to protect, what to avoid.
>
> One thing to flag: it works best with a regular cycle (28–32 days). If your cycle is irregular right now, the card might not be accurate — happy to hear what you'd need for it to work for your situation.
>
> Completely free, no account, one question to start: [https://phlow.work]
>
> Let me know if it's useful or if it misses — genuinely want to know either way.

**Why it works:** Sets honest expectations about cycle regularity upfront — avoids disappointment and builds trust. Invites her to articulate what she'd need (product discovery). Does not assume her condition makes her a fit.

---

### Type 6 — Freelancer / solopreneur
*Use for: women who work for themselves, no employer, their schedule is self-managed.*

> [Name] — you know how you don't have an HR buffer? Your schedule is just your schedule?
>
> I built something for people exactly like that. It's called Phlow — it maps your hormonal cycle to your work calendar so you're not accidentally burning out your best cognitive window on admin.
>
> Free, no account, 10 seconds. Curious what you think: [https://phlow.work]

**Why it works:** Opens by naming a reality she knows well (no HR buffer). Makes the product feel built specifically for her situation. Doesn't over-explain.

---

### Type 7 — Community DM (women founder Slack/Discord, colder)
*Use for: women you interact with in a community but haven't spoken with 1:1. This one is the most careful — communities hate cold product pitches.*

> Hi [Name] — I noticed your post about [something specific she said]. I'm not pitching you anything, I promise — but I built a free tool that tries to solve for exactly what you described, and I'd love an honest reaction from someone who gets it.
>
> Phlow: work-planning card based on your hormonal cycle. One question, 10 seconds, no account. [https://phlow.work]
>
> If it doesn't land, I genuinely want to know why.

**Why it works:** References something specific she said — this is not a blast, it's a response. "I'm not pitching you anything, I promise" defuses the guard immediately. Asking for critique is disarming in a community that's used to being sold to.

## Follow-up message (use once, 3–5 days after, if no reply)

*Use for: anyone who didn't reply. Send once. Never twice.*

> Just following up once in case this got buried — curious if you had a chance to see it.  
> [https://phlow.work]  
> No worries if not your thing.

**Rule:** One follow-up only. If still no reply, they're not the right person right now. Move on.

---

## After they try it — the reply that gets you real data

When someone tries Phlow and messages back, your job is NOT to explain more. It's to listen.
Reply with one of these:

**If they say it's accurate / landed:**
> That's exactly what I hoped to hear — what phase were you in when you opened it? And did it match anything you'd been feeling?

**If they say it didn't land:**
> Thank you, that's really useful. Can I ask — was it the phase description that didn't fit, or the task recommendations? Or just felt too vague?

**If they say "interesting but...":**
> What would make it actually land for you? I'm in the early feedback stage — genuinely want to know.

These three follow-up questions generate the language research you need for copy.

## What to track (simple, no tool needed)

After each DM, note down:

```
Contact: [Name]
Date sent: [date]
Type: [1–7]
Response: installed / no reply / replied without installing / installed + feedback
Their reaction in their own words: [quote if any]
```

Keep this in a note, a spreadsheet, a voice memo — whatever you'll actually use.

After 20 DMs, review:
- How many installed? (pass condition: > 5)
- What language did they use in their reaction?
- Which message type converted best?
- Any quote worth using in copy?

---

## The signal you're looking for

You're not looking for "this is great." You're looking for:

> *"Wait — I'm in mid-luteal right now and I've been IN the zone all week."*  
> *"I just showed this to my co-founder."*  
> *"I haven't thought about it this way before but it makes total sense."*  
> *"Can I use this with my team?"*

Those are the aha moments. Screenshot them. They are your first content, your first social proof, and your first copy.
