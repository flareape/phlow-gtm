## The Short Answer

**Run TikTok as your primary discovery channel. Run Instagram as a zero-cost repurpose of TikTok carousels. Never create original content for Instagram. And do not post daily — it will cost you signal clarity without adding proportional return.**

Here's why.

---

## Where Your Audience Actually Lives

Women founders and freelancers 30–45 are on Instagram. They are on it more consistently than TikTok, and the relationship-first algorithm means that when your content resonates there, it travels peer-to-peer — a friend shares your carousel in DMs, not the algorithm. That's actually the most powerful distribution for Phlow, because Phlow's users refer naturally to other women who track their cycles. Instagram enables that word-of-mouth chain better than TikTok does.

But here's the problem: Instagram does not hand you cold reach. It rewards existing relationships and accounts that already have trust built. You have zero history there. On TikTok, a brand new account can reach 800 people in 48 hours with the right hook — as post #33 proved, even with the wrong audience hook. That cold discovery function is what you need right now, because your founding audience hasn't found you yet.

So the architecture is: **TikTok is where strangers find you. Instagram is where women who already found you save your content and forward it to their friends.**

---

## The Repurposing Logic

Carousels are already 9:16. What you produce for TikTok carousel is already an Instagram carousel. The repurpose workflow is:

1. Post carousel to TikTok
2. Download the video (without watermark — use SnapTik or TikTok's own save)
3. Post to Instagram Reels as carousel — same slides, same caption with minor adaptation (remove TikTok-specific phrasing, add 3–5 hashtags relevant to founder/productivity/women)
4. Done. 15 minutes of additional work per post.

The Instagram caption should be slightly edited — not because the content is different, but because the Instagram audience is warmer and more likely to save. Lead with the emotional payoff earlier. The TikTok caption earns attention in the first sentence. The Instagram caption can assume the viewer already cared enough to tap.

**What you do NOT do:** Film original content for Instagram. Never create an Instagram-first post. Instagram gets everything second. If it flops there, you learn nothing new. If it performs, you get bonus distribution.

---

## On Daily Posting

Daily posting is a trap at this stage and the data already shows you why.

You have three posts. Post #17 (face-to-cam, Day 26) got 295 views but 3 saves and 20 likes — that's a **6.8% engagement rate** and a **1% save rate** against a relatively small reach. Post #33 (carousel, builder hook) got 833 views but only 9 likes — the algorithm distributed it but the audience was wrong. The save rate is lower; the resonance is lower.

Those are two completely different signals. One reaches more people but doesn't land. One reaches fewer people but lands correctly. If you had posted 7 times that week instead of once, you'd have no idea which of those patterns was repeating and why. Volume would have buried the signal.

Your job right now is not to post as much as possible. Your job is to post enough to find out which hook format, which emotional angle, and which opening line consistently drives saves from the right women. That learning is only visible if you give each post enough time to breathe and enough of your mental energy to actually analyze what happened.

**The right cadence for a solo founder at signal stage: 3 posts per week on TikTok, 3 repurposed to Instagram.** That's 3 learning cycles per week. It's sustainable. It's enough for the algorithm to learn your account. And it leaves you enough brain space to actually read the comments and DMs that tell you who is watching.

---

## The Real Conversion Engine Right Now

One thing worth naming directly: LinkedIn is already driving 10% of your onboarding traffic from a single post. One post. That's your warm network — women who already know you or know of you, who trust you enough to click through and use the app. That conversion rate will be much higher than anything TikTok sends you for months.

This doesn't mean abandon TikTok. It means: **protect your LinkedIn energy too.** That channel is currently doing more per post than any social platform you're running. LI-2 should go out this week or next — it's already drafted and the trigger conditions are met.

## The Minimum Viable Strategy, Stated Plainly

| Channel | Role | Cadence | Content source |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | Cold discovery, hook testing | 3x/week | Original |
| Instagram | Warm repurpose, peer-to-peer shares | 3x/week | TikTok repurpose only |
| LinkedIn | High-intent conversion, founder network | 1–2x/month | Original, separate voice |

**What counts as a win per post (revised targets for this stage):**
- TikTok: >5 saves per post. Not view count — saves. A post with 200 views and 8 saves beat one with 1,000 views and 3 saves every time.
- Instagram: >10 saves. Instagram audience saves more passively, so the bar is higher.
- LinkedIn: track profile clicks and direct installs in the first 48 hours.

---

## What Post #34 Should Teach You

"I used to think I was lazy. It was day 26." — this is the right hook for the right audience. The user lens, not the builder lens. Watch the saves rate on this one relative to #33. If saves/views is higher than #33, that confirms the direction: **lead with the woman's experience, not the product's origin story.** The founder-building-in-7-days hook does not reach the woman who downloaded Phlow because she couldn't write a single email on a Thursday. Those are different people with different TikToks.

The moment you have 3 posts that consistently hit >1% save rate with the user hook, you've found your format. That's when you consider increasing to 4–5x/week — not before.
